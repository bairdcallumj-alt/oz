Story Overview

The original story follows Dorothy as she leaves Kansas, travels through Oz, meets companions, faces danger, and discovers that the answers she seeks are not quite where she expected them to be.

1. Kansas

Dorothy is a young girl who lives with her Aunt Em and Uncle Henry on a farm in Kansas. One day, a powerful cyclone lifts her house into the air and carries it far away to the magical Land of Oz.

When the house lands, it accidentally crushes the Wicked Witch of the East, freeing the people known as the Munchkins from her rule.

Think about How does the cyclone force Dorothy into a new world before she is ready?
2. The Yellow Brick Road

Dorothy wants to return home, but nobody knows how to get back to Kansas. The Good Witch of the North tells her that the only person who may be able to help is the mysterious Wizard of Oz, who lives in the Emerald City.

Dorothy sets off along the Yellow Brick Road to find him. The road becomes more than a path through Oz; it becomes the beginning of her search for help, safety, and answers.

Think about Why do stories often use roads or journeys to show change?
3. New Friends

Along the way, Dorothy meets three companions. The Scarecrow wishes for a brain because he believes he is not intelligent. The Tin Woodman wants a heart because he thinks he cannot feel emotions. The Cowardly Lion wants courage because he believes he is afraid of everything.

Together they travel through forests, rivers, fields, and dangerous lands while helping one another overcome many challenges.

Think about What do the companions reveal about self-doubt and confidence?
4. The Wizard

When they finally reach the Emerald City, the Wizard agrees to help them, but only if they defeat the Wicked Witch of the West. After a difficult journey, Dorothy defeats the witch and returns to claim the Wizard's promise.

However, the travellers discover that the Wizard is not a powerful magician at all. He is an ordinary man who has been using tricks and illusions to appear great.

Think about What happens when powerful people or places are revealed to be less magical than they seemed?
5. There’s No Place Like Home

Although the Wizard cannot magically give the Scarecrow, Tin Woodman, and Lion what they seek, he helps them realise they already possess the qualities they wanted. The Scarecrow has shown wisdom, the Tin Woodman has shown kindness, and the Lion has shown bravery throughout their journey.

Eventually Dorothy learns that she has always had the power to return home. Using the magic of her silver shoes, she travels back to Kansas, carrying with her new friends, new experiences, and a greater understanding of herself.

Think about What does Dorothy understand at the end that she did not understand at the beginning?
