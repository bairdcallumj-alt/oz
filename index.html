The road north

This resource begins with a familiar story: a young person leaves home and enters a world that seems larger, brighter, and more powerful than anything she has known before.

In Hinemoa’s version, the journey is shaped by post-war change, Māori urbanisation, family pressure, work, ambition, and the quiet cost of trying to fit into a new world. The city glitters, but glitter can hide as much as it reveals.

Students can first meet the original story, then explore how Oz has shaped popular culture, before reading Hinemoa’s journey as a new text with its own setting, symbolism, and questions.
